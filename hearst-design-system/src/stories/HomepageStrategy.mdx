# Homepage Layout Strategy

**Four** structural layout templates—Curator, Mosaic, Stream, and Editorial—support conversion, return visits, and time-on-site across 29 Hearst brands. **Editorial Modes** (Live, Feature, Service) sit on top: they are the behavioral layer (density, which modules run, ad pressure) while layouts define **arrangement**. See <a href="?path=/docs/docs-editorial-modes--docs">Docs / Editorial Modes</a> for the full mode framework.

## The Four Layouts (Structure)

These are **templates**, not brand personalities. *Who* the reader is and *how urgent* the day feels come from **editorial mode** and scheduling—see Editorial Modes. Each layout is a different way to **arrange** modules and set a default information architecture.

<LayoutCard name="A: The Curator" subtitle="NYT-inspired editorial hierarchy" brands="Default for **Live** on many news-forward brands: Esquire, Cosmopolitan, Elle, Harper's Bazaar, Town & Country">

**Structure:** 60/40 hero split, numbered top stories, clear vertical hierarchy, room for a trending bar and high story count.

**Fits** when the job is *ranking and scanning* (timeliness, "what’s new"). Modes can still soften or intensify: Feature Mode on Curator is *possible* with fewer stories and different modules—less ideal than The Editorial for immersion, but valid for brand constraints.

**Core principle (structural):** A single, editor-led path down the page—few parallel surfaces, strong story order.

</LayoutCard>

<LayoutCard name="B: The Mosaic" subtitle="Verge/TIME-inspired modular grid" brands="**Versatile** — strong for **Live, Feature, and Service**; Elle, Harper's Bazaar, House Beautiful, Veranda, Delish">

**Structure:** Bento-style hero, category tabs, content grid, editor’s picks carousel. High *surface area* without a single long scroll column.

**Fits** visual browsing, seasonal storytelling, and task-oriented users who self-select a lane (tabs). The same grid can be dialed to **high density** (Live) or **larger cards** (Feature) via mode presets.

**Core principle (structural):** Many entry points, clear categorization, exploration over a single story arc.

</LayoutCard>

<LayoutCard name="C: The Stream" subtitle="Mobile-first engagement feed" brands="Default for **Service** on many utility/lifestyle brands: Good Housekeeping, Delish, Prevention, Women's Health, Men's Health">

**Structure:** Single column, sticky compact header, alternating card types (image-right, full-bleed, text, commerce, etc.), room for shopping carousel and continuous scroll.

**Fits** goal-oriented and social-driven sessions—*find, skim, act*. Service Mode is the natural pairing; other modes are possible (e.g. **Live** in a feed) but scan worse than The Curator for "catch me up."

**Core principle (structural):** Momentum. Rhythm beats hierarchy.

</LayoutCard>

<LayoutCard name="D: The Editorial" subtitle="Magazine-style hero, stacked secondaries" brands="Default for **Feature** on many prestige titles: weekend Esquire, Cosmopolitan, Elle, Harper’s Bazaar, Town & Country">

**Structure:** Dominant portrait hero, stacked secondary stories, room for *fewer* top slots and more *visual* breathing room. Newsletter and longform can lead without competing with a wall of small headlines.

**Fits** lean-back, showcase, and runway-adjacent moments. Poor match for *high urgency* "breaking" jobs unless intentionally artistic.

**Core principle (structural):** One hero moment; everything else supports it.

</LayoutCard>

## Conversion Levers

Each layout can deploy multiple conversion mechanisms at different scroll depths. The goal is to offer at least 3 newsletter/subscribe opportunities per session without creating banner blindness.

<Callout emoji="⚙️" title="Editorial Mode overrides">
**Live, Feature, and Service** turn modules on or off and change *which* newsletter variant appears (e.g. Live uses a **sticky** CTA instead of inline; Feature emphasizes **full-width** signup). A layout’s “default levers” below describe the **template**; always cross-check the active **mode** in <a href="?path=/docs/docs-editorial-modes--docs">Editorial Modes</a> for the live configuration.
</Callout>

## Why Four Layouts (And Three Modes) Instead of One

A single homepage template optimizes for one *structure* at the expense of others. **Modes** then re-weight the same (or a different) template for the job of the day. Hearst's 29 brands span different reader *tasks* and *schedules*:

**News-forward brands** (Esquire, Cosmo, Elle) often default to **Live** on **The Curator** for the weekday *catch-up*—clear hierarchy, high story count. The same brand may run **Feature** on **The Editorial** for weekend longform.

**Visual-first brands** (House Beautiful, Veranda, Delish) often use **The Mosaic** so readers can *browse by image* and self-filter by category; mode shifts density (e.g. fashion week = Feature, recipe season = Service).

**Lifestyle/utility brands** (Good Housekeeping, Prevention, Men's Health) often default to **Service** on **The Stream**—social/search arrival, goal-oriented scroll, shopping and categories.

**Showcase and prestige** moments (print-adjacent, runway, magazine drops) map naturally to **The Editorial** under **Feature Mode**, even when the same brand uses **The Curator** the rest of the week.

## Implementation Notes

All four layout templates share a common component library and token system. Switch **structure** with `HomepageByLayout` or **behavior** with `HomepageByMode` (applies mode presets, then you may override the layout).

```tsx
<HomepageByLayout layout="curator" />  // "mosaic" | "stream" | "editorial"

<HomepageByMode mode="live" />
<HomepageByMode mode="feature" layoutOverride="mosaic" />
```

Layouts automatically adapt to the active brand's tokens (colors, typography, spacing) through the `ThemeProvider`. No layout-specific token overrides are needed. See <a href="?path=/docs/docs-editorial-modes--docs">Editorial Modes</a> for the preset table and brand scheduling examples.

### Recommended A/B Testing Plan

1. **Phase 1:** Deploy The Curator to Cosmopolitan and Esquire. Measure newsletter signup rate and 7-day return rate against current homepage.
2. **Phase 2:** Deploy The Mosaic to House Beautiful and Elle. Measure pages per session and ad viewability.
3. **Phase 3:** Deploy The Stream to Good Housekeeping and Men's Health. Measure scroll depth and time on page.
4. **Phase 4:** Cross-test -- try The Stream on Cosmopolitan, The Curator on House Beautiful. Identify if brand audience or layout structure has greater impact.
